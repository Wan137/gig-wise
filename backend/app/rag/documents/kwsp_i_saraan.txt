== What it is ==

i-Saraan is a voluntary contribution initiative by EPF, designed to help
self-employed individuals and gig economy workers without fixed incomes obtain
Government incentives to grow their retirement savings.

== Benefits ==

Special Incentive: a special incentive of 20% (up to a maximum of RM500) of
the total EPF voluntary contributions made for the current year. Lifetime
incentive limit of RM5,000, or when the member turns 60, whichever is earlier
(subject to Government terms and conditions). The RM5,000 lifetime limit took
effect from 2024, based on contributions made from 2024 onwards.

Annual Dividend: annual EPF dividends on top of the contributed savings.

Death Assistance: subject to EPF terms and conditions.

Tax Relief: eligible for tax relief, subject to LHDN/IRB terms and conditions.

== Eligibility ==

- Malaysian citizen.
- Self-employed individual (earns income from their own work, not employed by
  others), an individual with no fixed income, a civil service member under a
  pension scheme, or a worker in the gig economy sector.
- Registered EPF member.
- Below 60 years of age.

Civil service employees under a pension scheme, and serving military members,
are both explicitly eligible if they are below 60.

== Contribution limits ==

- Minimum: RM1 (subject to payment channel).
- Maximum: RM100,000 per year. This is the combined maximum across all
  Voluntary Contribution channels (i-Simpan, i-Saraan, i-Saraan Plus, i-Suri,
  and Akaun Persaraan Top-Up Savings) across all payment channels combined,
  not RM100,000 per channel.

== How much to contribute for the maximum incentive ==

To qualify for the maximum annual special incentive of RM500, a member needs
to contribute at least RM2,500 per year under i-Saraan (the incentive is 20%
of contributions, capped at RM500; RM2,500 x 20% = RM500).

Illustration from KWSP: a member contributing RM1,000 in the year receives
RM200 special incentive; a member contributing RM2,500 or RM5,000 in the year
both receive the capped RM500 special incentive (since 20% of RM2,500 already
hits the RM500 cap).

== How the lifetime cap is calculated ==

The lifetime maximum is the cumulative total of annual Special Incentives
received, capped at RM5,000 or until the member turns 60, whichever comes
first. Example from KWSP: a member receives the special incentive in both 2024
and 2025 (RM500 each year) - total accumulated special incentive is RM1,000,
leaving RM4,000 of lifetime incentive still available (RM5,000 - RM1,000).

== How contributions are distributed across EPF accounts ==

i-Saraan contributions are split into EPF accounts by this ratio:
- Akaun Persaraan (Account 1): 75%
- Akaun Sejahtera (Account 2): 15%
- Akaun Fleksibel (Account 3): 10%

For members who have reached age 55, 100% of contributions go to Akaun Emas
instead. The special incentive itself is credited to Akaun Persaraan (Account
1) for members below 55, or to Akaun Emas for members 55 and above. The
special incentive is credited to the member's account twice a year, subject to
receipt of the incentive payment from the Government.

== Payment channels ==

One-time or recurring (Auto Simpan) contributions via: KWSP i-Akaun app or
i-Akaun (Member) web portal; internet banking (Alliance Bank, Bank Islam, Bank
Muamalat, BSN, CIMB, Hong Leong, Kuwait Finance House, Maybank, MBSB, Public
Bank, RHB, AmBank); bank agent counters (BSN, Maybank, Public Bank, RHB - RHB
accepts debit card/transfer only); registered bank agent (EB) BSN; debit card
via EPF Mobile Team / Relationship & Advisory (RA) services; or Self-Service
Terminal (SST) at EPF offices.

Contribution payments are credited to the member's EPF account within 3
working days for electronic payments, or 7 working days for other channels.

== Registration ==

Register via the KWSP i-Akaun app, at any Self-Service Terminal (bring MyKad),
at an EPF office (Form KWSP 16G (M) - "Permohonan Pendaftaran Caruman Sukarela
i-Saraan/i-Suri"), or online via i-Saraan Online Registration on the EPF
website.

== Important notes ==

- All i-Saraan contributions paid to EPF are final - no refunds.
- Contributions made after a member reaches age 55 are credited into Akaun
  Emas and can only be withdrawn once the member reaches age 60.
