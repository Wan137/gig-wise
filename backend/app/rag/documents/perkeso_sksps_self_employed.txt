== Overview ==

The Self-Employment Social Security Scheme (LINDUNG KENDIRI) was introduced to
provide protection for individuals who are self-employed under the provisions of
the Self-Employment Social Security Act 2017.

In the beginning, this scheme is compulsory for the self-employed in the
Passenger Transportation Sector which are taxi, e-hailing and bus drivers under
the provisions of the Self-Employment Social Security Act 2017, which took
effect on 1 June 2017.

Effective from 1 January 2020, the coverage of the scheme was extended to 19
other sectors, making it 20 sectors covered by the scheme.

The coverage for self-employed individuals contributing to the scheme starts
from the date and time the contribution is paid and recognised on the
contribution payment receipt.

== Self-employed sectors covered (20 total) ==

1. Passenger Transportation Sector (taxi, e-hailing, bus drivers)
2. Goods or Food Transport (includes food delivery / p-hailing riders)
3. Agriculture
4. Livestock
5. Forestry
6. Fisheries
7. Food (food production/preparation)
8. Construction
9. Manufacturing
10. Hawkers
11. Accommodation Premises
12. Online Business
13. Information Technology
14. Data Processing
15. Agents
16. Professional Services
17. Support Services
18. Arts
19. Household Services
20. Beauty and Healthcare

== Eligibility ==

- Malaysian citizen / permanent resident, without age limit.
- Individuals who work for themselves to earn a living.

Required documents: photocopy of identity card (IC); copy of driving licence /
e-hailing profile / permit / certificate, or a letter of confirmation from the
relevant association or agency, depending on the sector.

== What the scheme covers ==

This scheme provides protection for self-employed insured persons against
employment injuries, including occupational diseases and accidents during
work-related activities. Self-employment injury means personal injury to a
self-employed insured person caused by an accident or an occupational disease
arising out of and in the course of their self-employment activity, including
while travelling for the purpose of that activity. Occupational disease is a
disease caused by or arising from any occupation specified in the Fifth
Schedule of the Employees' Social Security Act 1969.

== Benefits provided by PERKESO ==

Medical Benefit: free medical treatment at PERKESO's panel clinics/government
hospitals for a self-employment injury or occupational disease, or
reimbursement of expenses incurred at non-panel clinics (subject to PERKESO's
conditions / the Fees Act 1951).

Temporary Disablement Benefit: paid for the period the self-employed insured
person is on medical leave certified by a doctor for not less than 4 days
(including the day of the accident); a medical certificate is required. Rate:
minimum RM30.00 per day, maximum RM105.33 per day.

Permanent Disablement Benefit: available to a self-employed insured person who
suffers permanent disability due to a self-employment injury. Rate: 90% of the
selected insured monthly earning. Example calculation of the lump-sum payment
(assessment 20%, age factor 8873 for below 20 years of age, contribution
RM592.80): (RM3,950 x 90% - 30 days) x 20% x 8873 = RM210,290.10.

Constant-Attendance Allowance: paid to a self-employed insured person with total
permanent disablement who is so severely incapacitated as to constantly
require the personal attendance of another person (certified by a Medical
Assessor or the Appellate Medical Board). Fixed at RM500 per month.

Dependants' Benefit: if a self-employed insured person dies as a result of a
self-employment injury, dependants are entitled to a payment of 90% of the
selected insured monthly earning. Eligible dependants: widow/widower and
children under 21. If none: (a) parents, (b) brothers/sisters below 21, or (c)
grandparents. Widow/widower, parents and grandparents receive lifetime
benefits; children receive the benefit up to age 21 or marriage (whichever is
earlier), or until completion of a first degree if in higher education.

Facilities for Physical/Vocational Rehabilitation: provided free of charge.
Physical rehabilitation includes physiotherapy, occupational therapy,
reconstructive surgery, prosthetics/orthotics and other appliances (wheelchair,
crutches, hearing aids, spectacles, special shoes, etc). Vocational
rehabilitation includes courses such as sewing, radio repair, etc.

Funeral Benefit: paid to the eligible person if the self-employed insured
person dies as a result of a self-employment injury, or while receiving
periodical Permanent Disablement Benefit. Amount: actual cost incurred or
RM3,000 (effective from 1 June 2024), whichever is lower.

== Contribution Plan (Rate of Contribution, Self-Employment Social Security
Scheme, Act 789) ==

There are exactly four insured-earning tiers. A self-employed person selects
the tier closest to their actual monthly earning; the contribution amount is
fixed per tier, not a percentage of income.

| No. | Insured Monthly Earning | Contribution Per Month | Contribution Per Year |
|-----|--------------------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| 1   | RM1,050                  | RM13.10                  | RM157.20                 |
| 2   | RM1,550                  | RM19.40                  | RM232.80                 |
| 3   | RM2,950                  | RM36.90                  | RM442.80                 |
| 4   | RM3,950                  | RM49.40                  | RM592.80                 |

Registration and contribution payment can be made via the Prihatin app or the
Prihatin Web portal.

== SPS Padanan Caruman (government contribution-matching grant) ==

Separate from the standard contribution table above, PERKESO has periodically
run a targeted matching-grant programme ("SPS Padanan Caruman" / "Geran
Padanan Caruman SKSPS") for vulnerable self-employed groups, under which the
Government subsidises a majority share of the Plan 2 contribution (RM232.80
per year) and the individual pays the remainder (for example, one PERKESO
announcement stated the Government financed 80% (RM186.20) and the target
group paid 20% (RM46.60) of the Plan 2 contribution). This grant is
budget-dependent, targeted at specific groups, and not a standing entitlement
for every SKSPS contributor - do not assume it applies without checking current
PERKESO eligibility criteria for the relevant year.
